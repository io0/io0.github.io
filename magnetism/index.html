What is magnetism?

A group of forces which "add" to each other in the same way but "add" to other forces in a different way.

Few materials have "intrinsic" magnetism that can be felt in a macroscopic setting. These are the metal poster children: iron, nickel.

You can destroy ferromagnetism by striking it with a hammer!

The idea of putting charges closer together or father apart to vary the force felt at a distance — changing the "zoom" level of the system.
