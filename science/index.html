He says the two problems with neural interfaces are interconnections and packaging. He says Neuralink was a brutal lesson that no matter how thin you make the wires, how clever you are, you should never put things in the brain. There's just no space there. The needle for insertion is tiny, yet it causes damage. (I also heard that the electronic components don't move in the same way that biological material does in the brain, causing repeated scarring over time — I don't remember if it was Max who said so though.)
